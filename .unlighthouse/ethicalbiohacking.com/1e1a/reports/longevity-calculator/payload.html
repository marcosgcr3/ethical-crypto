The Science Behind Biological Age

Your biological age is not fixed. Research shows it can accelerate or reverse based on lifestyle choices — here's what the evidence says.

Epigenetic Clocks

DNA methylation patterns (Horvath clock, DunedinPACE) can measure cellular aging with high accuracy. Your lifestyle directly reprograms these molecular switches.

Sleep & Telomere Length

Chronic sleep deprivation accelerates telomere shortening — a key marker of cellular aging. Seven to eight hours of quality sleep correlates with significantly longer telomeres.

Read more →

Exercise as Anti-Aging Medicine

Regular aerobic and resistance training activate sirtuins, reduce senescent cells, and have been shown to lower biological age by 3–10 years in longevity studies.

Read more →

Time-Restricted Eating

Intermittent fasting triggers autophagy — cellular self-cleaning — and activates longevity pathways including mTOR inhibition and AMPK activation.

Read more →

Stress & Cortisol Aging

Chronic psychological stress accelerates epigenetic aging. Studies show that consistent mindfulness practice can reduce biological age by 1–3 years within 12 months.

Longevity Supplementation

NAD+ precursors (NMN/NR), resveratrol, and spermidine support mitochondrial function and have demonstrated age-reversal effects in multiple human and animal studies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is biological age?
Biological age measures how old your body's cells and systems actually are — based on lifestyle, genetics, and biomarkers — as opposed to your chronological age (years since birth). It can be significantly younger or older than your calendar age.
How accurate is this calculator?
This tool provides a lifestyle-based estimate using research-validated factors. For a clinical-grade result, consider epigenetic clock tests (DunedinPACE, Horvath clock) available through longevity clinics and direct-to-consumer labs.
Can you actually reverse your biological age?
Yes — multiple studies confirm this. Fahy et al. (2019) demonstrated a 2.5-year reversal in 1 year using diet, exercise, sleep, and supplements. See our Longevity section for actionable protocols.
How often should I retake this assessment?
Every 90 days as you implement changes. Significant lifestyle improvements — especially in sleep and exercise — can measurably shift your biological age in 8–12 weeks.
