MAGNESIUM: THE MOLECULAR BRAKE PEDAL

Magnesium is an essential cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions, but its most critical role in the 2026 sleep model is its ability to modulate GABA receptors and regulate the NMDA receptor complex. In simple terms, your brain stays awake because of "Excitatory Glutamate." Magnesium sits inside the NMDA receptor like a plug, preventing glutamate from over-firing your neurons.

GLYCINE: THE THERMAL SWITCH FOR SLEEP ONSET

Glycine is a simple, non-essential amino acid that performs a biological miracle for sleep architecture. As established in circadian science, your body cannot enter deep sleep until your core temperature drops by approximately 1°C. Glycine facilitates this by triggering peripheral vasodilation.

By increasing blood flow to the hands and feet, Glycine helps "dump" internal heat into the environment. Clinical trials using 3 grams of pure Glycine powder have demonstrated that subjects reach Slow-Wave Sleep significantly faster and report lower levels of daytime fatigue, even if total sleep time remained constant. Furthermore, Glycine acts as an inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brainstem and spinal cord, reducing nocturnal movements that fragment sleep.
